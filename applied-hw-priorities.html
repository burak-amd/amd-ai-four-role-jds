The Role

We are hiring an Applied AI Engineer to apply AI to top engineering priorities across AMD silicon programs. You will deepen hardware engagements by scaling proven PPA optimization patterns into supporting AI tools for RTL generation and optimization, and by building AI-assisted RTL debugging and analysis for acceleration-focused teams (including programs such as Arcadia). You will work with architects and methodology leads to generalize capabilities across product generations (including Camelot and future roadmap families) so improvements compound rather than reset each program. A portion of the role may align with recursive self-improvement for compute: closed loops where models and tooling measurably improve their own training or verification efficiency subject to strict governance.

The Person

You ship. You prefer tight feedback loops with silicon owners, versioned datasets, and dashboards over slide-only narratives. You can read RTL, reason about timing and power budgets, and partner with CAD and PD when flows break.

Key Responsibilities

  • Design, train, and deploy models and agents for RTL authoring assistance, micro-architecture exploration, and PPA-aware refactoring under signoff constraints
  • Build debugging assistants for RTL and integration issues surfaced by acceleration teams—root-cause hypotheses, trace summarization, and safe edit suggestions with verification hooks
  • Scale internal and partner-inspired PPA workflows into reusable services, datasets, and evaluation harnesses adopted by multiple SoC programs
  • Partner with research and infra on RSI-for-compute pilots: automated curriculum generation, regression selection, or tool-time optimization with human approval gates
  • Maintain production hygiene—monitoring, rollback, data lineage, and security review—for any customer- or partner-touching components

Preferred Experience

  • Experience shipping ML or LLM-based tools to non-ML stakeholders in hardware or systems organizations
  • Familiarity with RTL (Verilog/SystemVerilog), simulation, lint, or formal basics; exposure to PD/timing concepts a plus
  • Experience with distributed training, inference optimization on GPUs, or CI integration for hardware repos

Academic Credentials

Outcomes

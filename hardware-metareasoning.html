The Role

We are hiring an AI Research Scientist to solve core research problems so AI works for hardware engineering—not only for software-centric benchmarks. You will focus on metareasoning (when to invoke which tool, model, or simulator), reward shaping under partial observability, and learning under high- and mixed-latency rewards typical of physical design, verification, and silicon bring-up. You will split time between internal HW programs and research that generalizes hardware engineering across projects, platforms, and future generations.

The Person

You are excited when the “environment” is a toolchain that takes hours or days to return a score. You decompose long-horizon problems into learnable structure, credit assignment, and uncertainty-aware control. You partner respectfully with RTL, PD, and verification experts and treat their time as a scarce signal.

Key Responsibilities

  • Develop models and algorithms for metareasoning over EDA and internal simulation APIs—budgeting expensive steps, selecting abstractions, and recovering from tool failures
  • Research reward shaping and auxiliary objectives that remain faithful to tapeout-relevant metrics when full evaluation is too slow for inner-loop learning
  • Characterize and improve learning under high and mixed latency (batch jobs, queueing, seasonal cluster contention) including off-policy learning, world models, and asynchronous RL
  • Co-own problem definitions with silicon teams; ensure research artifacts plug into AMD’s internal AI-for-HW roadmap, not only one-off demos
  • Publish at top venues where appropriate; contribute internal courses and documentation that lift adjacent teams

Preferred Experience

  • PhD or equivalent in ML, planning, RL, or related; strong record on long-horizon or sparse-reward problems
  • Exposure to hardware development flows (RTL, synthesis, place-and-route, timing, power, or verification)
  • Experience with LLM tool use, agents, or hierarchical RL in real engineering settings
  • Comfort with large-scale experimentation and statistical rigor when rewards are noisy

Academic Credentials

  • PhD in Computer Science, Machine Learning, or related field strongly preferred.

Outcomes

  • Latency-aware learning — demonstrate measurable sample-efficiency gains on at least one internal HW task where reward latency exceeds one hour p50
  • Generalization — method or benchmark package adopted on two distinct silicon programs (e.g. different IP blocks or process nodes)
  • Metareasoning — deployed policy or planner that reduces wasted EDA cycles by a documented double-digit percentage on a pilot flow

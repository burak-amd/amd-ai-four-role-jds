The Role

We are hiring an AI Research Scientist specializing in reinforcement learning to drive RL research for strategic partnerships—including deep customer collaborations with frontier AI labs (for example Anthropic, OpenAI) on problems such as kernel optimization, PPA (power, performance, area) optimization, and code optimization under realistic silicon constraints. You will own research threads where reward signals are subtle or gameable: reward hacking research, robust evaluation, and alignment between learning objectives and true downstream silicon quality. You will translate partnership learnings into methods and benchmarks AMD can reuse internally.

The Person

You treat partnership research as a product: tight hypotheses, reproducible harnesses, and clear go/no-go criteria. You are skeptical of naive reward proxies and enjoy building defenses—verification layers, held-out physical checks, and human expert gates—without killing learning efficiency.

Key Responsibilities

  • Lead RL formulations for kernel, runtime, and codegen-style optimization tasks arising in co-development programs with strategic AI partners
  • Co-define PPA and power/thermal objectives with partner and AMD silicon teams; build reward models and curriculum learning strategies that resist shortcut exploitation
  • Research reward hacking: detect and mitigate spurious optima when proxies substitute for full signoff flows (timing closure, power integrity, functional verification sampling)
  • Publish or contribute technical artifacts where partnership agreements allow; represent AMD credibly in joint technical forums
  • Partner with RL infrastructure and applied teams to ensure experiments scale on AMD’s AI compute fleet and feed back into internal toolchains

Preferred Experience

  • PhD or equivalent in ML, RL, or related field, with publications at venues such as NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, or equivalent applied RL track record
  • Hands-on RL at scale (policy optimization, preference-based RL, or related) for code, compilers, or systems
  • Familiarity with hardware metrics (PPA, timing, power) or willingness to ramp quickly alongside silicon experts
  • Experience in constrained or safety-aware RL, reward modeling, or adversarial evaluation of learned policies

Academic Credentials

  • PhD in Computer Science, Machine Learning, or related field strongly preferred.

Outcomes

  • Partnership milestones — deliver agreed RL research milestones on co-innovation programs with measurable PPA or kernel metrics on reference designs
  • Anti-hacking — ship at least one evaluation or regularization package adopted by partner and internal teams to catch reward exploits before tapeout-risking commits
  • Reusability — internal benchmark or environment derived from partnership work reused by ≥2 AMD programs outside the originating deal

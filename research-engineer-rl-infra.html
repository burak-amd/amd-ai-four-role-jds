The Role

We are hiring an AI Research Engineer to own reinforcement learning infrastructure at scale: distributed policy and value training, rollout generation, logging, checkpointing, and researcher-facing APIs on large GPU fleets. You make RL scientists productive by improving throughput, fault tolerance, reproducibility, and observability—turning fragile notebooks into reliable systems that RSI, generalizing-HW, and RL research programs depend on.

The Person

You profile before you optimize; you treat researcher time as expensive as GPU time. You communicate SLAs, capacity plans, and incident patterns clearly and partner on cost–quality tradeoffs.

Key Responsibilities

  • Design and implement distributed RL training stacks (data parallel, pipeline parallel, or hybrid) integrated with AMD’s schedulers and storage
  • Build high-throughput rollout workers, trajectory stores, and reward computation pipelines with versioning and audit trails
  • Instrument jobs for debugging (NaNs, stragglers, OOMs), implement autoscaling and preemption-safe checkpointing
  • Collaborate with research scientists on experiment templates, hyperparameter sweeps, and safe promotion paths from research to wider team use
  • Drive reliability: on-call rotations, runbooks, and postmortems for infra incidents affecting RL training

Preferred Experience

  • Master’s or PhD in CS or related, or Bachelor’s with strong systems track record in ML platforms
  • Deep experience with PyTorch (or JAX), NCCL/MPI-style distributed training, and GPU cluster orchestration
  • Prior ownership of RL training infra, LLM post-training pipelines, or large-scale experiment management
  • Proficiency in C++/Python performance tuning, I/O optimization, and containerized workloads

Academic Credentials

  • Bachelor’s degree required; Master’s or PhD preferred for research-heavy collaboration depth.

Outcomes

  • Throughput — documented increase in effective RL training throughput (tokens or rollouts per GPU-hour) on flagship internal workloads
  • Reliability — meet agreed SLOs for job success rate and mean time to recovery on the RL training platform
  • Adoption — self-service RL training templates used by all three scientist tracks (RL, RSI, generalizing HW) with positive researcher satisfaction scores
